A tribute to Richard Stallman

The funniest of the Free Software movement's Founding Fathers

Wikipedia defines Richard Matthew Stallman -often known by his initials rms- an American free software movement actmonokaiivist and as a programmer. He campaigns for software to be distributed in a manner such that its users receive the freedoms to use, study, distribute and modify that software. He campaigns for software to be distributed in a manner such that its users receive the freedoms to use, study, distribute and modify that software (aka. Free Software's four essential freedoms).

rms has received numerous recognitions for his work, including:
  • 1990 - The Association for Computing Machinery's Grace Murray Hopper Award "For pioneering work in the development of the extensible editor EMACS (Editing Macros)"
  • 1996 - Honorary doctorate from Sweden's Royal Institute of Technology
  • 1998 - Electronic Frontier Foundation's Pioneer award
  • 2001 - Honorary doctorate, from the University of Glasgow
  • 2004 - Honorary doctorate, from the Universidad Nacional de Salta
  • 2007 - First Premio Internacional Extremadura al Conocimiento Libre
  • 2016 - Honorary doctorate, from Pierre and Marie Curie University
"My work on free software is motivated by an idealistic goal: spreading freedom and cooperation. I want to encourage free software to spread, replacing proprietary software that forbids cooperation, and thus make our society better."
- Richard Stallman
